Myth of the Stars: More Constellations

Andromeda

           Andromeda was the daughter of King Cepheus and Queen Cassiopeia. Her mother had boasted that she is more beautiful than the Nereids, the sea nymphs. Poseidon, the sea god, did not like that, so he sent a sea monster to ravage the area that she lived on. The only way to save the island was for Andromeda to be sacrificed to the sea monster. She was chained up to a cliff and waited for her doom. Before she could be killed, Perseus came and saved her. After he saved her, they got married.


Aquila

           Aquila was Zeus’s personal eagle. He would do a variety of jobs for him. He was the one who brought the prince that would become Aquarius to Olympus.


Boötes

           Boötes was the son of a mortal and Demeter. He invented the wagon and the plough. After he died Demeter wanted him as a constellation.


Canis Major and Minor

           Canis Major and Canis Minor were the dogs of Orion.


Cassiopeia

           Cassiopeia was the queen Ethiopia and wife of Cepheus. They had a daughter named Andromeda. Cassiopeia boasted that their daughter was more beautiful than the Nereids. Her boasting caught the attention of Poseidon. He was furious at this boasting, so he sent a sea monster to ravage the land that she and her husband ruled. They were told that the only way to save their people was to sacrifice their daughter. Luckily for them Perseus came along and saved their daughter.


Cepheus

           Cepheus was the king Ethiopia and husband of Cassiopeia. They had a daughter named Andromeda. His wife boasted that their daughter was more beautiful than the Nereids. Her boasting caught the attention of Poseidon. He was furious at this boasting, so he sent a sea monster to ravage the land that they ruled and the only way to save their people was to sacrifice their daughter. Luckily for them Perseus came along and saved their daughter.


Cetus

           Cetus is the sea monster that Poseidon had sent to kill Andromeda and was slain by Perseus.


Cygnus

           Cetus is the sea monster that Poseidon had sent to kill Andromeda and was slain by Perseus.


Delphinus

           Delphinus is a dolphin in the service of Poseidon. He helped Poseidon in persuading Amphitrite to be his wife. When the time came Poseidon made him into a constellation.


Draco

           Draco is the dragon that was protecting the golden apple tree that Hercules came to retrieve. After he was slain, he was put into the sky.


Hercules

           Hercules was the son of Zeus and Alcmene. In a fit of insanity, due to Hera being angry at him, he killed his wife and children. The only way for him to make amends was to complete twelve labors. The labors are killing the Nemean Lion, killing the Hydra, capture the Cerynitian Hind, capture the Erymanthian Boar, clean out the stables of a king, killing the Stymphalian Birds, capture the Cretan Bull, capture Diomedes’s man eating horses, get the belt of Hippolyte, capture Geryon’s cattle, fetch golden apples that were guarded by a dragon, and fetch Cerberus.


Hydra

           Hydra is one of the monsters that Hercules killed during his twelve labors. Hera felt bad for the monster, so she put it among the starts as a constellation.


Lepus

           Lepus was a rabbit that was being hunted by Orion.


Lyra

           Lyra is a musical instrument that was invented by Hermes. Later Apollo started to use the instrument in his music.


Ophiuchus

           Asclepius was a demigod son of Apollo who was raised by Chiron after his mother died. When he died, he was made into the constellation Ophiuchus and also made into the a god of medicine.


Orion

           Orion was a giant who hunted many beasts, such as Taurus, Lepus, and Scorpius. He had two dogs who also became constellations. They are Canis Major and Canis Minor. There are a few versions of his death. One is where he was killed by Scorpius. In another Artemis fell in love with Orion, but Apollo killed him. In that version of the myth Orion had went blind at one point.


Pegasus

           Pegasus and Chrysaor, a boar, were born when Perseus cut off Medusa’s head. Pegasus assisted in various myths, such as helping in the fight against a chimera.


Perseus

           Perseus was the son of Zeus and Danae, who was the princess of Mycenae. His grandfather did not want him to be born due to a prophecy that foretold that Danae’s son would kill him. He tried to kill Danae and Perseus by locking them in a chest when Perseus was only a toddler before throwing them out to sea. The two of them were eventually found by a fisherman named Dictys. Eventually Perseus grows up and fights a variety of monsters with the help of the gods. He kills Medusa and keeps her head as a trophy. He ends up using Medusa’s head to turn Atlas to stone when he was not hospitable to him. He also uses Medusa’s head to help him fight off a sea monster that had plans on eating Andromeda. Perseus ends up marrying Andromeda afterward.


Pleiades

           In one version of the Orion myth, the Pleiades were sisters who were being sought after by Orion.


Ursa Major and Ursa Minor

           Calisto was seduced by Zeus and gave birth to a son named Arcas. Zeus transformed them into bears and were later hunted down. After they died Zeus put them into the sky and renamed them Ursa Major and Ursa Minor.
